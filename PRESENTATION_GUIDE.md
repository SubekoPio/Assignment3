## 🎤 Presentation Format Options

### Option A: Individual Segments (Recommended)
Each presenter stands and presents their full 8-12 minute section, then sits down. 
Next presenter stands for their section.

**Pros**: Clear structure, easy transitions, individual visibility
**Cons**: More standing/sitting, might lose momentum

### Option B: Sequential Presentation
All 5 presenters stand together at the front. Only the current presenter speaks 
while others listen attentively (visible support).

**Pros**: Unified team presence, continuous energy, shows teamwork
**Cons**: Might be cramped space, harder to see all presenters

### Option C: Hybrid Approach
Two presenters stand at a time. Current presenter speaks, next presenter gets ready.
They alternate standing.

**Pros**: Balance of both approaches
**Cons**: More movement, slightly distracting

**Recommendation**: Option A (Individual Segments) for clarity and professionalism

## 🚀 Final Tips for Success

### For the Team:
- **Support each other**: Show enthusiasm during others' presentations
- **Know your material**: Practice until it feels natural
- **Anticipate questions**: Think about what might be asked
- **Stay professional**: Dress appropriately and speak clearly
- **Show unity**: You're a team, not 5 individuals

### For Each Presenter:
- **Make eye contact**: Engage with your audience
- **Use hand gestures**: Point to slides and use natural movements
- **Vary your tone**: Don't monotone—show enthusiasm
- **Pause for questions**: Invite interaction
- **Manage time**: Stick to your 8-12 minute target
- **Have backup answers**: Be ready for tough questions

### For the Room:
- **Test technology early**: Don't wait until presentation time
- **Arrange seating well**: Audience can see all presenters
- **Have water available**: Hydration is important for speaking
- **Remove distractions**: Phone silent, focus on presentation
- **Introduce presenters clearly**: "Next up is [Name]..."
